# Expert Vocal Terminology (Technical Dictionary)

## 1. Registration & Configuration

- **Chest-Dominant (M1)**: Thick vocal fold configuration, rich in low harmonics.
- **Head-Dominant (M2)**: Thin vocal fold configuration, airy or flute-like high notes.
- **Mix (Passaggio)**: The transition zone where chest and head resonance overlap.
- **Vocal Fry**: The lowest register, characterized by a slow, popping vibration.
- **Whistle Register**: The highest human register, producing flute-like sounds.

## 2. Technical Stylizations

- **Belting**: Powerful, high-intensity singing with a chest-like quality in high ranges.
- **Twang**: Narrowing of the epiglottic funnel to create a bright, "brassy" resonance.
- **Appoggio**: The classical technique of breath support and management.
- **Distortion / Rasp**: Intentional noise added to the signal via false vocal fold vibration.
- **Vibrato (Oscillation)**: Natural rhythmic pulsation in pitch (ideal speed: 5-7 Hz).
- **Legato**: Smooth, connected phrasing without audible breaks between notes.

## 3. Spectral & Anatomical

- **Formant Tuning**: Adjusting vowel shapes to boost specific spectral frequencies.
- **Subglottal Pressure**: The air pressure below the vocal folds required for phonation.
- **Glottal Strike**: A sharp onset of sound caused by rapid pressure release.
- **Laryngeal Positioning**: High (bright/tense) vs. Low (dark/relaxed) larynx.
- **Spectral Centroid**: The "center of mass" of a sound's frequency spectrum.
