# Professional Vocal Adjectives (Forensic Thesaurus)

## 1. Brightness & Color (Timbral)

- **Luminous**: Shimmering, radiant, bright without being harsh.
- **Bell-like**: Pure, ringing resonance, often associated with clear soprano or tenor ranges.
- **Piercing**: Sharp, focused, cutting through dense mixes.
- **Gilded**: Rich, golden, warm yet distinctly bright.
- **Velutinous**: Velvety, smooth, with a rich dark-chocolate color.
- **Resonant**: Deeply vibrating, full-bodied, filling the acoustic space.

## 2. Texture & Grain

- **Gritty**: Raw, textured, with visible vocal "dirt" (fry or rasp).
- **Crystalline**: Perfectly clear, transparent, no audible noise or friction.
- **Ethereal**: Airy, light, breathy, floating above the music.
- **Brittle**: Thin, sharp, fragile, often used to describe specific high-tension styles.
- **Coarse**: Rough, thick, heavily textured (often for rock or blues).
- **Smoky**: Dark, slightly breathy, lingering and evocative.

## 3. Power & Compression

- **Robust**: Strong, healthy, energetic, and stable.
- **Visceral**: Deeply felt, raw power, hitting the listener physically.
- **Compressed**: Tense, dense, focused energy with high subglottal pressure.
- **Effortless**: High power without visible strain, fluid and natural.
- **Supple**: Flexible, bending easily, capable of quick dynamic shifts.
- **Athletic**: Demonstrating high control, range, and technical prowess.
